The history of esports
                   

The earliest forms of competitive gaming can be traced back to the 1970s, with events like the "Intergalactic Spacewar Olympics" in 1972 at Stanford University. The prize was a year's subscription to Rolling Stone magazine. The 21st century marked a significant consolidation of esports, with the professionalization of teams, coaches, and training regimens, mirroring traditional sports. Platforms like Twitch and YouTube became crucial for broadcasting and fan engagement.

Here are some games that are played at esports levels:

Dota 2

Dota 2 esports is a highly competitive global scene centered around Valve's strategic 5v5 MOBA, culminating annually in The International, a premier tournament renowned for its massive, crowdfunded prize pools.

Valorant

Valorant esports is a rapidly growing global competitive scene for Riot Games' tactical first-person shooter, featuring teams of five agents with unique abilities battling for dominance in a round-based objective mode across structured international leagues and major tournaments like Champions.

League of Legends

League of Legends esports is a global professional scene where teams of five skilled players strategically compete in the multiplayer online battle arena to destroy the opposing team's Nexus for significant recognition and prizes in organized leagues and the annual World Championship.

Rocket League

Rocket League esports is a professional competitive scene where highly skilled teams and players compete in the vehicular soccer game for significant prizes.


Overwatch

Overwatch esports is an open ecosystem for Blizzard's hero-shooter, Overwatch 2, where teams compete in regional and international Overwatch Champions Series (OWCS) tournaments for prize pools and recognition.

Fortnite

Fortnite esports is a competitive battle royale scene focused on individual or duo performance in Epic Games' free-to-play shooter, featuring large-scale tournaments like the Fortnite Champion Series (FNCS) where players compete for significant prize pools by being the last one standing.

Counter strike

Counter-Strike esports is a highly strategic and long-standing global competitive scene for Valve's tactical first-person shooter, where two teams of five compete to plant or defuse a bomb, with the prestigious Valve-sponsored Major Championships being the pinnacle of competition.

Brawl stars

Brawl Stars esports is a thriving competitive scene for the mobile 3v3 multiplayer battle game, featuring organized tournaments like the annual Brawl Stars Championship where teams compete globally for significant prize pools.
